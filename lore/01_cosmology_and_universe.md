# 01 — Cosmology and Universe

## 1.1 The World of Vyrr

Vyrr is the name the deepest tablets give the planet. The word predates the Inversion. In the **Old Vesari** tongue it means roughly "the open palm" — a metaphor for a world that once held its life on an outward surface, beneath a real sky. No living being remembers that world. None of the surviving peoples remember a sky.

What remains is the **Hollow** — Vyrr's inverted interior. Geography did not vanish in the Inversion; it folded. Continents are still there, but pressed inward, layered in concentric strata around the captive sun.

## 1.2 The Aphelion Sphere

At the center of the Hollow hangs the **Aphelion Sphere** — a fragment of Vyrr's original sun, captured during the Inversion and held aloft by forces no surviving science can explain. It is roughly the size of a small moon, casts cold golden light across the entire inner world, and pulses at a rhythm called the **Aphelion Beat** — once every twenty-three breaths. All life inside the Hollow has, over eons, synchronized its own pulse to the Beat. When the Beat falters, fauna sicken. When the Beat skips, biomes wither.

The Aphelion is dying. Hairline fractures called **Suncracks** have spread across its golden shell over the last six centuries. Each Suncrack leaks parasitic motes called **Hollowlings** that drift down through the strata, infecting flora and corrupting the **Aeon Sovereigns** who once kept the strata stable.

The Aphelion is also the source of the **Echo-Walkers**. When the world's danger reaches a threshold, the Aphelion spins a fragment of itself into a resonant body and casts it to the Anchor.

## 1.3 The Anchor

The Anchor is the single fixed point of stability inside the Hollow — a small plateau of unfolded ground roughly equidistant from the Aphelion and the outermost strata. Echo-Walkers awaken on the Anchor. The Anchor is where they build, where they store, where they return to when they die. It is also where the **Resonance Loom** stands, a half-buried construct of pre-Inversion origin that no surviving civilization built. The Loom hums in time with the Aphelion. Touching it for the first time is how an Echo-Walker learns its name.

The Anchor is canonically why the player has a "home base." It is also why death is not permanent: the Aphelion re-spins the Walker from the Loom. There is a cost — every respin costs the Aphelion a sliver of light. This is foreshadowing.

## 1.4 The Strata

The Hollow is layered. The folded geography of pre-Inversion Vyrr was pressed inward into nine concentric **strata**, each one a sealed chambered band of biomes wrapped around the Aphelion. Strata are not perfectly closed — over eons, water, fauna, and survivors carved tunnels between them. The descent is real but not vertical-in-the-old-sense; Echo-Walkers experience it as digging *inward*.

The nine strata are detailed in [04_biomes.md](04_biomes.md). In summary:

1. **The Root Hollows** — outer crust, soft loam and shale, where the Anchor sits
2. **The Glasswright Reaches** — pale clay and crystal veins
3. **The Vesari Necropolis** — pre-Inversion tombs and ruins
4. **The Sunless Verdancy** — folded forests preserved in stasis
5. **The Drowned Aphelion** — an inland sea pulled into the Hollow
6. **The Emberforge Strata** — magma chambers and old forges
7. **The Salt Wastes of Dawning** — a buried desert
8. **The Auroric Veil** — a stratum of frozen light and aurora-storms
9. **The Final Spiral** — the innermost descent, immediately around the Aphelion

## 1.5 The Resonance Field

The Aphelion does not emit ordinary light. It emits a phenomenon the Glasswrights named the **Resonance Field** — a low harmonic that permeates the Hollow and gives matter inside the Hollow strange properties. Three principles arise from the Field:

1. **Form-anchoring.** Constructed objects (placed tiles, built walls, crafted items) acquire a low-grade permanence the natural world lacks. This is the in-world justification for player building.
2. **Echo-memory.** Strong emotional events leave residual imprints in the Field. Walkers can sometimes witness these as **Echoes** — silent ghost-scenes of past inhabitants. This is the lore justification for environmental storytelling and is detailed in [11_environmental_storytelling.md](11_environmental_storytelling.md).
3. **Resonant binding.** Certain materials, when smelted or pressed in resonance with the Aphelion Beat, become **bound matter** — usable in tools and armor. Materials grow more resonant the closer they originate to the Aphelion. This is the in-world justification for the material tier progression detailed in [09_relics_artifacts_progression.md](09_relics_artifacts_progression.md).

## 1.6 The Hollowlings

When a Suncrack opens, the Aphelion leaks. The leakage is not flame but **motes** — soft dim sparks that drift downward and accumulate in low places. Where they accumulate, they coalesce into **Hollowlings**: small, soft, vaguely insectoid parasites with no minds of their own. Hollowlings infect larger creatures. Infected creatures become more aggressive, lose appetite, and develop the characteristic gold-veined corruption visible in their sprites (this is a key visual identifier for "hostile mob" in later asset work).

When Hollowlings accumulate inside an Aeon Sovereign — the ancient guardians of each stratum — they consume the Sovereign's mind over centuries. The twelve corrupted Sovereigns the Echo-Walker will fight are the result of this process, detailed in [05_aeon_sovereigns_bosses.md](05_aeon_sovereigns_bosses.md).

## 1.7 Death and the Resonance Loom

Echo-Walkers cannot truly die inside the Hollow. When their resonant body is destroyed, the Aphelion respins them at the Loom. The cost is a sliver of Aphelion light per respin. The Aphelion has approximately **seventy-thousand slivers** remaining at the start of the game. This is a soft fail-state: the player will never reasonably exhaust it, but lorebooks and Sovereign dialogue track it, and one late-game NPC explicitly comments on the player's death count. ("You have been respun forty-one times, Walker. The sun grows dim because of mercies like yours.")

## 1.8 The Outside

The most important lore secret, revealed only in the final act:

There is no Outside. Or rather, there is, but it is not what the Vesari tablets claim. The pre-Inversion world was real, but the Inversion was not a natural cataclysm. It was a deliberate act, performed by a being whose name has been struck from every tablet. That being placed Vyrr in the Hollow on purpose, to hide it from something far worse than the Hollowlings. The dying of the Aphelion is the seal failing. The player's choice in the final act is whether to restore the seal (preserving the Hollow but ensuring no one ever leaves), break the seal (freeing the world but releasing what was hidden from), or attempt a third, unknown ending.

This final reveal is the keystone the rest of the lore must support without exposing too early.
