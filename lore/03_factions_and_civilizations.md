# 03 — Factions and Civilizations

The Hollow is a graveyard with a few embers still lit. This document catalogs every people, faction, cult, and remnant the player will encounter, hear about, or read in a tablet.

## 3.1 Living factions (encountered as NPCs)

### 3.1.1 The Lattice Survivors
**Status:** Living, scattered.
**Population:** Estimated 300–600 individuals across the entire Hollow.
**Stratum range:** Found in all strata from Root Hollows to Drowned Aphelion. None survive in deeper strata.
**Origin:** A patchwork people, descended from intermarried remnants of every collapsed civilization. They take their name from the "Lattice" — a network of marked tunnels their forebears mapped to allow safe travel between sealed enclaves during the Civilization Collapses.

**Gameplay role:** Most encountered NPCs are Lattice Survivors. Each is a craftsman, vendor, or quest-giver who arrives at the Anchor after specific progression triggers. Their personalities are individual; they are not a monolithic culture.

**Speech style:** Quiet, indirect. They use double negatives ("not unkind," "not displeased") because direct expression draws Hollowlings, in folk belief.

**Named individuals (sample, full roster in NPC manifest):**
- **Aelstren the Cartographer** — first NPC to arrive at the Anchor. Provides the world map. Speaks in halting, careful sentences. Lost a partner two centuries ago in the Salt Wastes; carries one of her gloves.
- **Brindle Quench-of-Coals** — Pyrenkin-descended smith. Arrives after the player smelts their first resonant ingot. Loud, given to swearing in pre-Inversion Old Vesari.
- **Mira-no-Last-Name** — a child who cannot remember her family. Arrives at the Anchor on her own. Acts as the storage clerk. Lattice Survivors discourage her direct expression but she ignores them.
- **The Cantor of Five Bells** — a wandering musician who teaches the Walker the Aphelion Beat as a musical pattern. Arrives in Act Two. Carries the only known intact bell from the Vesari Necropolis.
- **Old Hask** — a fisherman from the Drowned Aphelion. Arrives after the Walker explores the fifth stratum. Refuses to discuss what is in the deep water.

### 3.1.2 The Glasswright Remnant
**Status:** Living, sealed.
**Population:** Estimated 80–120 in a single sealed enclave deep in the Glasswright Reaches.
**Stratum:** Glasswright Reaches (2nd stratum).
**Origin:** Direct descendants of the Glasswright Concord who, when the Suppression Wars failed, retreated into a single sealed vault called the **Quiet Forge**.

**Gameplay role:** Encountered as a sealed quest hub after the player breaks a specific lore-gated door. They are the only living people who still practice resonant crafting at a pre-Collapse level of skill. They will not leave the Quiet Forge. The Walker can bring them materials in exchange for high-tier crafting unlocks.

**Speech style:** Formal, plural pronouns ("we craft, we measure, we wait"). They have not used singular pronouns for three thousand Beats as a cultural mourning rite for those they lost in the Wars.

### 3.1.3 The Listeners-Below
**Status:** Living, but ambiguous.
**Population:** Unknown. They may be one person hearing themselves echo, or hundreds. The truth is left deliberately unclear.
**Stratum:** Auroric Veil (8th stratum) and the Final Spiral (9th).
**Origin:** Either descendants of the original Aphelion Wardens who did NOT fall into the Sunken Diadem heresy, or solitary survivors driven mad by the Resonance Field at depth. Both hypotheses are presented in-game; the truth is never confirmed.

**Gameplay role:** Late-game NPCs encountered as masked, hooded figures in the deepest strata. They speak in riddles and trade in extremely rare items. They appear to know things they should not.

### 3.1.4 Wandering individuals
A small number of NPCs do not belong to any faction and have unique cultural backgrounds. They are documented individually in the NPC manifest. Examples:
- **The Veiled Buyer** — appears once per stratum, will buy any item for variable prices, never speaks except to name a number.
- **Korya the Returned** — claims to have been an Echo-Walker spun before the current era and forgotten by the Aphelion. Her claim is unverifiable. She may be lying.

## 3.2 Extant cults and antagonist organizations

### 3.2.1 The Sunken Diadem
**Status:** Active antagonist faction.
**Stratum range:** Final Spiral primarily; emissaries appear in all deeper strata.
**Origin:** The fallen Aphelion Wardens. After the dimming began, a faction within the priesthood developed a heresy: the Aphelion's death was *correct*. They came to believe the Aphelion was a tumor on the world, that the Hollow was a prison rather than a sanctuary, and that hastening the Aphelion's end was a holy duty.

**Members:** Robed cultists wearing rusted golden circlets ("diadems"). They have given up their names and answer only to fragments of their original titles ("the Third Reader," "the Hand-That-Once-Held-Light").

**Methods:** They do not openly attack the Walker until the deep game. Instead, they sabotage. They poison resonant springs. They break seals that should remain closed. They release Hollowlings into stable strata. The Walker uncovers their work through clue-trails, not direct confrontation, for most of the game.

**Leadership:** A masked figure called **the Diadem-Bearer** who is the final boss before the Aphelion itself.

### 3.2.2 The Hollowlings
**Status:** Non-sapient parasitic entities, but they exhibit emergent coordinated behavior.
**Origin:** Leaked Aphelion light, dispersed and curdled in low places. See [01_cosmology_and_universe.md](01_cosmology_and_universe.md) §1.6.

**Gameplay role:** Most hostile mobs in the game are creatures infected with Hollowlings, not Hollowlings themselves. Direct Hollowlings appear as boss-fight minions and as a rare swarm event called a **Mote Tide**.

### 3.2.3 The Wormbound
**Status:** Living-adjacent, debatable.
**Stratum:** Salt Wastes of Dawning (7th stratum).
**Origin:** A small group of Vesari refugees who, during the Civilization Collapses, allowed themselves to be inhabited by a benevolent symbiotic organism native to the buried desert. They became long-lived, slow, and impossible to corrupt by Hollowlings — but lost their individual identities over generations.

**Gameplay role:** The Walker may make a choice in the Salt Wastes regarding the Wormbound. They are not enemies. They are not allies. They are an option.

## 3.3 Extinct civilizations (referenced through ruins, tablets, and Echoes)

### 3.3.1 The First Vesari
The original pre-Inversion seafarers. Their cities are buried in the third stratum's Necropolis. Their language, **Old Vesari**, is the closest surviving people have to a lingua franca for the pre-Inversion world. Detailed in [10_language_glyphs_writing.md](10_language_glyphs_writing.md).

**Material culture:** Bronze-and-bone shipbuilding (folded into the Necropolis as the ships were never able to sail). Astronomical tablets carved with star maps that no longer reference any real sky. Coin-pieces strung as ceremonial garments.

### 3.3.2 The Aphelion Wardens (pre-Fall)
The priesthood who designed the Aphelion Working and survived the Inversion. Practiced in resonant theology, music as ritual, and the maintenance of the Aphelion. Their pre-Fall culture is preserved in the **Hymnal Vaults** in the Final Spiral — rooms full of intact musical instruments, tuning rods, and the sheet-glyphs of the Bright Aeon hymns.

### 3.3.3 The Glasswright Concord (pre-Sealing)
The civilization that perfected crystallized-Aphelion-light shaping. Their cities in the Glasswright Reaches are the most architecturally intact of the lost civilizations — most of the masonry is still vertical, just empty. The Walker can read murals depicting Glasswright life and learn the **Concord Script**.

### 3.3.4 The Pyrenkin Forgekeepers
The Hollowkin-descended forge-civilization who lived in the magma-tier sixth stratum. Their compact with Skoldur ended when Skoldur fell. Their cities are partially molten; the Walker recovers tools and ingots from cooling slag pools. Most Pyrenkin survivors were absorbed into the Lattice Survivors; Brindle Quench-of-Coals is their last full-blood descendant.

### 3.3.5 The Korumeni
A pre-Inversion civilization named on a single tablet. No ruins, no language, no other reference. May not have existed. May have been deliberately erased. Tablet K-I in the Vesari Necropolis is the only mention. This is a deliberate lore hook for a possible expansion or for player speculation.

### 3.3.6 The Drekhal
Same status as the Korumeni. Mentioned twice. No ruins survive. Tablets imply they were already gone before the Inversion.

### 3.3.7 "Those who left first"
Mentioned in the elision-language. A pre-Inversion group that "left" before the Inversion happened. The verb used is ambiguous: it can mean *departed*, *died*, or *transcended*. The Listeners-Below claim to know what happened to them. The Listeners-Below may not be trustworthy.

## 3.4 The Aphelion (itself)
Treated, in some texts, as a faction of one. The Aphelion has agency — it spins Echo-Walkers, it Beats, it dims. Whether it is sentient is a theological question the lore deliberately leaves unsettled.

The Glasswright Remnant believe the Aphelion is conscious and grieving.
The Sunken Diadem believe the Aphelion is a tumor pretending to be a sun.
The Lattice Survivors believe the Aphelion is a machine.
The Listeners-Below believe none of these and will not say what they believe.

The truth is revealed in the final act and is presented in the main story document.
