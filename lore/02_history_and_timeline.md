# 02 — History and Timeline

All dates are given in **Beats** (B), the Hollow's calendar unit, counted from the **First Recorded Beat** of the Aphelion after the Inversion. There is no surviving pre-Inversion calendar; pre-Inversion events are dated in **Pre-Beats (PB)** with deliberate uncertainty.

## 2.1 The Pre-Inversion Era (-∞ to 0 B)

What is known about pre-Inversion Vyrr is partial, mostly recovered from sealed tablets in the Vesari Necropolis. What follows is the surviving record. Most of it may be false.

### The Open Palm Age (~5000 PB onward)
Vyrr was a world with a sky. Its dominant civilizations were the **First Vesari** (sea-traders and astronomers) and the **Aphelion Wardens** in their original incarnation — a priesthood that studied the planet's sun without yet revering it. Three other civilizations are named on partial tablets but their tilesets, languages, and cities have not been found: the **Korumeni**, the **Drekhal**, and a people referred to only as "those who left first."

### The Long Counting (~800 PB to 100 PB)
Something approached Vyrr. The tablets do not name it. The First Vesari catalogued its arrival as a **counted darkness** that grew larger in the sky over seven centuries. The Wardens proposed the Aphelion Working — an experiment to fold the planet inward and seal it against the approaching thing.

### The Year of No Stars (~50 PB)
The sky went blank. The counted darkness had grown to occlude every other star. The First Vesari record this event with a phrase carved on hundreds of preserved stones: "the lamp before the lamp went out." Construction on the Aphelion Working accelerated. A figure named **only in elision** — the tablets carve a void where the name should be — directed the project.

### The Inversion Event (Year 0)
On a date Hollow scholars call **First Beat**, the Aphelion Working was triggered. Vyrr folded. The surface buried itself. The interior unfolded. A fragment of the sun was caught in the new center and became the Aphelion Sphere. The figure whose name is elided was the only being who knew exactly what had been done and why. They are presumed to have died at First Beat. The tablets are deliberately silent on this point.

## 2.2 The Bright Aeon (0 B – ~12,000 B)

The first twelve thousand Beats of the Hollow were prosperous. The Aphelion shone strongly. The Resonance Field was intact. Geography had been folded but most of it was still intact and habitable. The Hollow's twelve **Aeon Sovereigns** — beings that emerged spontaneously from the Resonance Field within each stratum — took up roles as guardians and stewards. The Sovereigns were not gods. They were closer to genius loci: powerful nonhuman intelligences bound to specific strata.

### The Glasswright Founding (~1,200 B)
Survivors of the First Vesari and a new people, the **Hollowkin** — adapted from the deep-cave dwellers who survived the Inversion in old tunnels — together founded the **Glasswright Concord** in the second stratum. The Glasswrights discovered that crystallized fragments of Aphelion light, called **clearstone**, could be shaped with resonant tools.

### The Vesari Continuity (~2,000 B – ~9,000 B)
The descendants of the First Vesari rebuilt a new society in the third stratum on the ruins of pre-Inversion cities. Calling themselves simply the **Vesari**, they preserved the tablet-record, built mausoleums to honor the buried surface, and developed the second of the Hollow's four written languages.

### The Forge Compacts (~4,500 B – ~11,000 B)
A Hollowkin offshoot founded a magma-tier civilization in the sixth stratum, becoming the **Pyrenkin Forgekeepers**. They struck a compact with **Skoldur**, the Aeon Sovereign of the Emberforge Strata, exchanging maintenance of his ember-rivers for the metallurgical knowledge that produced the strongest pre-corruption alloys.

### The Bloom Treaty (~7,300 B)
Auriax, Sovereign of the Sunless Verdancy, allowed a small population of pacifist Vesari to settle in the fourth stratum under a treaty preserved in surviving murals. This is the high-water mark of pre-corruption coexistence between Sovereigns and surviving peoples.

## 2.3 The Long Dim (~12,000 B – ~26,000 B)

The first Suncrack opened in the Aphelion roughly twelve thousand Beats after the Inversion. No one noticed for centuries. The first Hollowlings drifted unremarked through the upper strata.

### The First Madness (~12,800 B)
**Glaur-em**, the Sovereign of the Root Hollows, was the first to be infected. Its corruption was slow and outwardly indistinguishable from age. It began to demand tribute from the surface dwellers it had once protected. The Anchor plateau was abandoned by what few survivors lived nearby.

### The Suppression Wars (~14,000 B – ~17,000 B)
The Glasswright Concord and the surviving Vesari raised militias to fight what they believed to be a Sovereign that had simply gone bad. They did not yet understand the Hollowlings. They lost — Glaur-em's stratum was sealed off, and the upper Hollow shrank.

### The Recognition (~18,200 B)
A Glasswright scholar named **Mireth-of-the-Quiet-Forge** dissected a corrupted creature and isolated the first Hollowling for study. Mireth's surviving notes (Tablet Series M-IV, found in the Glasswright Reaches) are the foundational text on Hollowling corruption.

### The Falling of the Wardens (~21,000 B – ~24,000 B)
The **Aphelion Wardens** — the priesthood who had survived the Inversion and continued to tend the Aphelion remotely from the Final Spiral — turned. Whether they were corrupted, or whether their grief at the dying Aphelion drove them to a heretical theology that *welcomed* the dimming, the Hollow does not agree. They became the cult known as the **Sunken Diadem**, and they began to sabotage repair efforts.

### The Civilization Collapses (~24,000 B – ~26,000 B)
In rapid succession over two thousand Beats: the Pyrenkin Forgekeepers were overrun when Skoldur fell; the Glasswright Concord retreated into sealed enclaves; the Vesari became a ghost-civilization, their cities abandoned. The Bloom Treaty was broken when Auriax went mad. By 26,000 B, every Aeon Sovereign had been corrupted. The Hollow entered its present age.

## 2.4 The Hollow Age (~26,000 B – present, ~52,800 B)

Twenty-six thousand Beats of silent decline. The Aphelion's light has dimmed to roughly forty percent of its Bright Aeon intensity. Most strata are functionally abandoned by surviving peoples; the few who remain are detailed in [03_factions_and_civilizations.md](03_factions_and_civilizations.md).

### The First Spinning (52,801 B — Game Start)
At the start of the game, the Aphelion spins its first Echo-Walker in fifteen hundred Beats. The Loom hums. The Walker wakes on the Anchor. The player begins.

There is canonically a reason the Aphelion did not spin a Walker earlier. It is revealed in Act Three.

## 2.6 Notes for narrative designers

- **Never expose the elided figure's name in writing.** Every tablet, sign, sigil that mentions them must be visually censored — the censorship itself is a clue.
- **The Inversion was deliberate.** This is the keystone reveal. All pre-Act-Three lore should be ambiguous about cause.
- **The Aphelion is dying, but not because of failure.** This too is keystone. The dimming is a seal failing, not a battery emptying.
- **Civilizations did not all collapse for the same reason.** Each Sovereign's fall caused its civilization's collapse in a different way. This gives each biome's environmental storytelling a unique tragic shape.
