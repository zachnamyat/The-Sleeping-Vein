# 04 — Biomes: The Nine Strata of the Hollow

Each stratum is a concentric band of folded geography wrapped around the Aphelion Sphere. Strata are listed from outermost (where the Anchor sits) to innermost (where the Aphelion hangs). Each entry contains the canonical lore, the gameplay character of the biome, the dominant culture/history, and asset-design hints for later phases.

## Stratum 1 — The Root Hollows

**Position:** Outermost band.
**Lore name:** Old Vesari calls this stratum *vyrth-emar* ("the open earth").
**Setting:** Soft loam, shale outcroppings, root-networks from above-folded forests, scattered mushroom growths, slow groundwater seeping through cracks in the world-shell. The Anchor plateau is here. Most surviving people who live in the Hollow live here or in the upper Glasswright Reaches.

**Lore character:** This is the safest stratum — and the saddest. Hollowlings are rarest here, but the corruption of **Glaur-em** (the Aeon Sovereign) is most visible because the local fauna are simple and corruption shows starkly. The Lattice Survivors have small farmsteads in pockets of safe loam. Most have lost children to Hollowling-infected creatures.

**History:** This stratum was largely uninhabited even in the Bright Aeon — too close to the world-shell, too quiet. It became important only after the Collapses, when survivors retreated outward for safety.

**Gameplay character:** Starting biome. Slow combat, tutorial-tier mining, soft enemy density. The Anchor is here. Players will spend their first many hours in this stratum.

**Resources:**
- *Loam* (placeable tile, building material)
- *Shaleseed* ore (the tier-1 metal, lore-named "shaleseed" because it grows in shale veins like seeds)
- *Pale Cap* mushroom (food, weak healing)
- *Memory Root* (rare, used in early Echo-Walker upgrades)

**Boss:** Glaur-em the Engorged Stone-Father (see 05).

**Visual hints for asset phase:**
- Palette: warm tans, dim olives, deep blacks for cave walls, soft gold accents from filtered Aphelion light
- Lighting: soft, diffuse, "dim sunset under a high ceiling"
- Tilesets: organic, irregular, with occasional very-old worked-stone fragments hinting at pre-Inversion buried surface
- Sound: water drips, soft wind, distant low hum (the Aphelion Beat is faintest here)

## Stratum 2 — The Glasswright Reaches

**Position:** Second outermost.
**Lore name:** *Clearstone Reach* in Concord Script; the Vesari called it *baeloth-thirim* ("where light was shaped").
**Setting:** Pale clay walls, crystal veins running like roots through the stone, sunken Glasswright cities mostly intact, terraced workshops, foundries that have not been cold in three thousand Beats because the Quiet Forge still works.

**Lore character:** The crown jewel of post-Inversion civilization. The Glasswright Concord did more here than any other civilization did anywhere. Their architecture is geometric, modular, and obsessive about light: every building is oriented to catch the diffuse Aphelion glow on a specific surface at a specific Beat. Most of this still works. The Reaches glitter even in death.

**History:** Founded ~1,200 B. Civilization peak ~5,000 B to ~14,000 B. The Suppression Wars devastated the lower Reaches when the Concord attempted to fight Glaur-em above. The Quiet Forge has been sealed for roughly 3,000 Beats.

**Gameplay character:** Second biome. Crystal-tier mining, the first true ranged enemies, and the introduction of resonant crafting. The Quiet Forge is unlockable late in the player's first dozen hours.

**Resources:**
- *Clearstone* (tier-2 crystal, used for first resonant tool tier)
- *Concord Brick* (placeable, structural building material)
- *Veinglass shards* (used for early ranged ammunition)
- *Resonant tuning rod* (rare key item; required to access the Quiet Forge)

**Boss:** Vorr'kell the Tunnel Wyrm (see 05).

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: pale clay yellows, ice-blue crystal accents, soft pearl whites, deep crystalline blacks
- Lighting: prismatic, catching crystal veins; light leaks through walls in narrow rays
- Tilesets: geometric, modular, with intact Concord murals (see 11 for mural index)
- Sound: crystal chimes, distant hammering from the Quiet Forge, faint singing in Concord Script (key plot hook)

## Stratum 3 — The Vesari Necropolis

**Position:** Third stratum.
**Lore name:** *Threnos* in Old Vesari ("the place of remembered keels"). The Vesari did not call it a necropolis — that name came from the Glasswrights who first explored it.
**Setting:** A vast, dim, salt-encrusted stratum of folded coastline, with the upturned wrecks of Vesari ships fused into the stone. Mausoleums dot the landscape. Old quay walls run for miles, leading nowhere. The salt sticks to everything; tools rust faster here.

**Lore character:** Mournful. The Vesari were a people of the sea, and their entire culture was folded into a dry tomb at the Inversion. Most of their ships were docked. Many had crews aboard, waiting to sail at the Year of No Stars. The fossilized remains of those crews are interred in the mausoleums. This is where the Walker first encounters mass evidence of the Inversion's human cost.

**History:** The pre-Inversion Vesari built this coastline. The Inversion folded it intact. Vesari Continuity rebuilt on top of it during the Bright Aeon, then abandoned it during the Collapses. Now it is a layered ruin — pre-Inversion below, Continuity-era above, both empty.

**Gameplay character:** Third biome. Introduces undead-equivalent enemies (lore-named "Salt-Bound," not undead but rather Resonance Field imprints of Vesari sailors animating their own fossils). Tablet collection becomes a serious gameplay layer here.

**Resources:**
- *Saltbound iron* (tier-3 metal, lore-named because it was Vesari ship-iron preserved by the salt)
- *Mausoleum stone* (placeable, structural)
- *Tablet shards* (lore items)
- *Ship-bone scrimshaw* (rare, used for tool decoration / minor stats)

**Boss:** The Spawnmother of Carrion Hollows (see 05).

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: salt-white, weathered grey-green oxidized copper, dusty deep blue, occasional warm lantern orange
- Lighting: cold and even, like a museum
- Tilesets: layered — pre-Inversion architecture on the bottom, Continuity-era on top
- Sound: dry wind, distant bells (the Cantor's bell-types), occasional ship-creak (impossible, lore: it's the Resonance Field replaying)

## Stratum 4 — The Sunless Verdancy

**Position:** Fourth stratum.
**Lore name:** *Auriax's Bloom*, after the Sovereign who once protected it. The Vesari called it *vol-emin-vol* ("the green that lasts").
**Setting:** A folded forest, preserved in arrested vitality by Auriax's pre-corruption guardianship. Trees grow without true sunlight, sustained by the Resonance Field and Auriax's blessing. Massive root systems form bridges and walkways. Bioluminescent fungi replace what should be canopy light.

**Lore character:** This stratum is where the Walker first sees something *alive* on a large scale. The Verdancy is breathtaking and dying — Auriax's corruption has begun to twist the forest, but slowly. Wide swaths are still vibrant. The Walker may witness a single tree dying mid-game; this is intended as an emotional beat.

**History:** Folded intact at the Inversion. Stewarded by Auriax through the Bright Aeon. Settled briefly by Vesari pacifists under the Bloom Treaty (~7,300 B). The treaty held until Auriax fell. The Verdant settlement was overrun within decades.

**Gameplay character:** Fourth biome. Introduces poison/toxin mechanics. Wide-open spaces compared to earlier mining-corridor biomes; encourages exploration. First biome where the player can grow crops on placed Verdant soil.

**Resources:**
- *Heartwood* (tier-4 organic material, used for living tools and bows)
- *Spore-iron* (a metal that forms in the roots of mushroom colonies)
- *Verdant soil* (placeable, growable)
- *Auriax's Tear* (rare, drops only from corrupted Verdant fauna; foreshadowing item)

**Boss:** Sythrenn the Toxic Bloom (mid-biome) and Auriax the Verdant Tyrant (biome boss). (See 05.)

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: deep emerald, bioluminescent cyan and magenta, warm wood tones, slick black bark
- Lighting: many small light sources rather than one big one; mushroom glow
- Tilesets: organic curves, irregular root-bridges, hanging vines that move on the Beat
- Sound: insect chitter, wood creak, occasional birdsong from non-bird creatures (the Verdant fauna echo pre-Inversion sounds without being birds)

## Stratum 5 — The Drowned Aphelion

**Position:** Fifth stratum.
**Lore name:** *The Mirror Below* in Listeners-Below speech; *thrael-vaer* in Old Vesari ("the second sun").
**Setting:** An inland sea, pulled into the Hollow at the Inversion and held in place by the Resonance Field. The water is dark, dense, and shines with a faint internal light. Submerged ruins of a coastal Vesari city lie scattered in the shallows. Deeper trenches are bioluminescent.

**Lore character:** The Drowned Aphelion is mythologized by every surviving culture. It reflects the real Aphelion's light from below, creating the illusion that the world has two suns. The Vesari believed it was a piece of the original sun that fell *through* the world during the Inversion. (They were wrong, but they believed it.) Old Hask the fisherman refuses to discuss what is in the deep water for good reason.

**History:** The Vesari city of *Threnos-Below-Threnos* sits in the shallows. Its inhabitants drowned at the Inversion. Their remains have become Resonance Echoes that wander the water at certain Beats. This stratum has been visited least by every civilization because of the danger of the deep trenches.

**Gameplay character:** Fifth biome. Introduces swimming and underwater combat. Resource scarcity on land; players are forced into water to progress. First true open-environment biome (the previous four are corridor-dense).

**Resources:**
- *Tideglass* (tier-5 material, used for the first truly powerful resonant tools)
- *Drowned coral* (placeable, structural underwater building)
- *Brinepearl* (rare, used in resonant accessories)
- *Sunken Glyph fragment* (lore item)

**Boss:** Vol'thaar the Tide Sovereign (see 05).

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: deep navy, abyssal black, bioluminescent teal, warm gold-yellow for the Aphelion's reflection above the water surface
- Lighting: refracted, godrays through water, occasional Beats of brighter Aphelion light penetrating to depth
- Tilesets: drowned masonry, sand, soft coral, kelp-equivalents that wave on the Beat
- Sound: underwater muffled silence, distant whale-equivalent songs, the Aphelion Beat distinctly audible here

## Stratum 6 — The Emberforge Strata

**Position:** Sixth stratum.
**Lore name:** *Skoldur's Bellows*. The Pyrenkin called it *koreth-vaen* ("the long hearth").
**Setting:** Magma chambers, intact and ruined Pyrenkin forge-cities, slow ember-rivers, hardened slag plains, vents emitting heat-shimmers. The stratum is bright — not from the Aphelion, which is dim at this depth, but from the magma itself.

**Lore character:** This is the most physically dangerous biome the player has yet seen. The Pyrenkin civilization here was extensive, and most of it is gone — but enough survives that the Walker can see the silhouette of what was. Brindle Quench-of-Coals, when she arrives at the Anchor, has stories about specific forges in this stratum. Locating them and reactivating them is a sidequest chain.

**History:** Founded ~4,500 B. Reached technological peak around 9,000 B with the **Pyrenkin Compact** — formal pact with Skoldur. Collapsed catastrophically around 24,500 B when Skoldur fell; most Pyrenkin died in a single Beat when ember-rivers overflowed in coordinated unnatural floods.

**Gameplay character:** Sixth biome. Introduces fire/heat damage mechanics, smelting upgrades, and the first endgame-tier metalworking. Heat-resistance armor becomes mandatory.

**Resources:**
- *Ember-iron* (tier-6 metal)
- *Slag-glass* (tier-6 secondary, used for heat-resistant tools)
- *Pyrenkin charcoal* (a fuel that burns blue, used for resonant smelting)
- *Forge-Compact tablet* (lore item; locates the largest intact Pyrenkin forge)

**Boss:** Skoldur the Forge-Forsaken (see 05).

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: deep red, orange, black, slag-grey, gold trim on Pyrenkin masonry
- Lighting: dynamic, flickering, magma-driven — the biome has its own internal light source
- Tilesets: hardened lava, basalt, intact and ruined forge architecture (geometric, Pyrenkin-style with broad arches and short pillars)
- Sound: hot rock crackling, the distant roar of ember-rivers, occasional metallic ringing (impossible, lore: Resonance Echo of past forging)

## Stratum 7 — The Salt Wastes of Dawning

**Position:** Seventh stratum.
**Lore name:** *The Dawning* — named not for sunrise but for the strange grey false-light cast on the salt plains by some unknown source. The Vesari called it *naer-emar* ("the white that watches").
**Setting:** A vast buried desert, but not sand — *salt*. Flat white plains, salt-pillars, fossilized desert flora, intact pre-Inversion settlements partially salt-encrusted, slow-moving Wormbound colonies visible on the horizon.

**Lore character:** This stratum is the most alien of the upper biomes. The salt is not just salt — it has slight resonant properties, and at night (the Aphelion's dimmer phase) it glows faintly. The Wormbound are here, and the Walker's choice about them is one of the game's three major moral choices.

**History:** Pre-Inversion surface desert, folded inward. Settled briefly by Vesari refugees during the Collapses. The refugees became the Wormbound around 24,800 B. No other people have lived here since.

**Gameplay character:** Seventh biome. Introduces extreme heat by day, extreme cold by night (the Aphelion's Beat cycle is most felt here as a temperature swing). Wormbound encounters are non-combat by default; combat is a player choice.

**Resources:**
- *Saltbound steel* (tier-7 metal, lore-named: ordinary steel preserved in the salt for millennia and made resonant by long exposure)
- *Dawning glass* (a translucent salt-crystal used in late-game accessories)
- *Wormbound silk* (gift-only material, available only if the player makes the peaceful choice with the Wormbound)
- *Dawning relic* (rare drop from a unique encounter chain)

**Boss:** Naeren the Wandering Salt-Crown (see 05). Note: this boss is *optional* if the player makes peace with the Wormbound; in that path, an alternative challenge replaces it.

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: bone white, salt-glow soft cyan, warm sand-yellow, cold deep purple in shadowed crevices
- Lighting: flat and very bright in "day" Beats, dim and crystalline-glowing in "night" Beats
- Tilesets: salt flats, salt pillars, fossilized cactus-equivalents, partly-buried Vesari refugee architecture
- Sound: dry wind, faint salt-crystal chiming when stepped on, distant low Wormbound humming

## Stratum 8 — The Auroric Veil

**Position:** Eighth stratum.
**Lore name:** *The Singing Frost* in Listeners-Below speech; *vyaeloth* in Old Vesari ("the cold that sings").
**Setting:** A stratum of frozen aurora-storms. The temperature is near pre-Inversion arctic levels — supernaturally cold. Static patterns of frozen aurora hang in the air, refractive and slowly moving. Vast hollow ice-caves carved by aurora-wind. No civilization lived here.

**Lore character:** The Auroric Veil is where the Resonance Field becomes visible to the naked eye. The Field manifests here as the frozen auroras. Walking through them produces fragmentary Echoes — visions, half-glimpsed scenes. The Listeners-Below begin to appear here, ambiguous and masked. The Veil's lore is heavy with the approaching keystone reveal.

**History:** Uninhabited. The first Aphelion Wardens explored it briefly during the Bright Aeon and built a small **Hymnal Vault** here. The Vault is intact and the player may enter it.

**Gameplay character:** Eighth biome. Introduces cold-damage mechanics, Echo-vision sequences (mandatory lore exposure), and the first appearances of Sunken Diadem agents in person. Enemy density is lower but enemies are individually much more dangerous.

**Resources:**
- *Auroric ice* (tier-8 material; ice that does not melt and holds resonance permanently)
- *Frozen aurora-shard* (used for the highest pre-endgame tool tier)
- *Hymnal sheet-glyph* (lore item; teaches the Walker a piece of the Aphelion Hymn)
- *Listener token* (rare, traded by Listeners-Below)

**Boss:** Veyl-Aurora, the Singing Choir (see 05) — a multi-phase boss representing a collective rather than a single being.

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: deep indigo, frozen cyan, white-hot aurora green, cold magenta
- Lighting: the auroras themselves are the primary light source; Aphelion light barely reaches here
- Tilesets: ice walls, frozen aurora-veils as semi-transparent foreground elements, intact Hymnal Vault architecture in places
- Sound: a continuous, soft, choral hum that follows the Beat; sharp ice-cracks during combat; Listener whispers

## Stratum 9 — The Final Spiral

**Position:** Innermost. The Aphelion is visible from any open chamber.
**Lore name:** *The Diadem*. Old Vesari did not name this place — they never went here.
**Setting:** A descending spiral of vaulted golden chambers around the Aphelion Sphere itself. The Sunken Diadem's stronghold. Hymnal Vaults, Aphelion Working ruins, the **Resonance Loom's Twin** (a second Loom, broken), and finally the chamber of the Aphelion. Light is everywhere, but cold and dim.

**Lore character:** The endgame. Every keystone reveal happens here. The Diadem-Bearer rules from a throne built into the wall of the Aphelion's chamber. The elided figure's name is recoverable here, if the Walker pieces together every clue. The Aphelion is encounterable as a being.

**History:** The original site of the Aphelion Working. The original headquarters of the Aphelion Wardens. The seat of the Sunken Diadem after the Fall. The architecture is pre-Inversion at its core, with layers of Bright Aeon and Diadem-era construction on top.

**Gameplay character:** Endgame. Multiple bosses in sequence. The player's choice between three endings is made here. Mechanics introduced earlier all return at maximum difficulty.

**Resources:**
- *Aphelion shard* (true endgame material; recovered only from defeated Diadem enforcers and from broken Suncracks)
- *Diadem-gold* (tier-9 metal, the highest crafting tier)
- *Resonant essence* (rare, used in the final ending choice)
- *The elided name* (lore item; literally a fragment of a name; not equipped or used, but its possession changes one ending)

**Bosses:** The Diadem-Bearer (mandatory) and The Aphelion (optional, ending-dependent). See 05.

**Visual hints:**
- Palette: pale gold, cold white, deep cathedral black, blood-red Diadem banners, occasional pre-Inversion bronze
- Lighting: omnidirectional Aphelion glow; light feels like cold sun through stained glass
- Tilesets: vast, geometric, cathedral-like, with Pre-Inversion machinery built into the walls
- Sound: the Aphelion Beat at maximum volume; layered choral hum from every direction; the player's footsteps echo abnormally
