# 09 — Relics, Artifacts, and Progression Tiers

This document defines the canonical material progression, the named key relics, and the lore-justified reason each tier exists. It is intended to be the source of truth for crafting designers, asset designers, and balance designers.

## 9.1 The Resonant Tier System

All crafted equipment in Aetherdeep is **bound matter** — material that has been smelted, pressed, or shaped in resonance with the Aphelion Beat. The tier of bound matter is determined by where in the Hollow the source material originated. Material closer to the Aphelion holds resonance more strongly and yields stronger tools, weapons, and armor.

The tier ladder, from outer to inner:

| Tier | Material name | Stratum origin | Lore signature |
|------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| 0 | Bare-Hand | n/a (Walker's own resonance) | Soft golden glow |
| 1 | Shaleseed | Root Hollows (1) | Dull warm-grey, faint pulse |
| 2 | Clearstone | Glasswright Reaches (2) | Pale prismatic, audible chime |
| 3 | Saltbound iron | Vesari Necropolis (3) | Salt-mottled grey-blue, slow resonance |
| 4 | Heartwood + Spore-iron | Sunless Verdancy (4) | Living-feeling, warm to the touch |
| 5 | Tideglass | Drowned Aphelion (5) | Deep-blue translucent, hums |
| 6 | Ember-iron | Emberforge Strata (6) | Red-hot at forge, warm permanent |
| 7 | Saltbound steel | Salt Wastes (7) | Mirror-white, "watching" feel |
| 8 | Auroric ice | Auroric Veil (8) | Cold-blue, sings on the Beat |
| 9 | Diadem-gold | Final Spiral (9) | Pale gold, weighs more than it should |

Bound matter at higher tiers is not strictly *better* — it is more *resonant*, and resonant matter affords more sophisticated crafted properties (multi-affix items, conditional bonuses, etc.). The game's combat balance treats tier as a soft progression curve, not a hard ceiling.

## 9.2 Crafting station progression

Mirroring the material ladder, crafting stations also advance in tier. Each higher tier unlocks at the corresponding biome's first major recipe.

1. **Loam Bench** — basic worktable, placed by the Walker shortly after waking.
2. **Clearstone Forge** — built once the Walker recovers enough Clearstone; light prismatic foundry.
3. **Saltbound Anvil** — built from recovered Vesari ship-iron and Necropolis stone.
4. **Verdant Cradle** — a living crafting station with bark walls; bonds with Heartwood.
5. **Tideglass Loom** — partially submerged; crafts Tideglass items.
6. **Pyrenkin Bellows** — recovered Pyrenkin forge unit; the Walker may install one at the Anchor.
7. **Salt-Crown Press** — slow, ritual-aided station; crafts saltbound steel goods.
8. **Auroric Anvil** — cold-resonance station; can only function within range of Aphelion light.
9. **Diadem Reliquary** — endgame station; converts recovered Sunken Diadem materials into Diadem-gold artifacts.

These can be visually stacked on the Anchor as the player progresses; visually, the Anchor becomes a layered cathedral of the Walker's descent.

## 9.3 Key Relics

These are unique items that gate progression, deliver lore, and serve specific narrative beats. Quantities are not random; each exists exactly once in the game.

### 9.3.1 Stone-Father's Pulse
- **Source:** Glaur-em (Boss 1).
- **Function:** Repairs a damaged Anchor section, opens stratum 2 descent.
- **Lore:** Glaur-em's slow heart, still beating faintly even in death. The Anchor receives it as a grafted organ.

### 9.3.2 Vorr'kell's Lantern
- **Source:** Vorr'kell (Boss 2).
- **Function:** Illuminates dark passages; required for several mid-Reaches and deeper exploration paths.
- **Lore:** Vorr'kell's twisted lantern-head, gently re-tuned by the Glasswright Remnant.

### 9.3.3 Coral Veil
- **Source:** The Spawnmother (Boss 3).
- **Function:** Allows short-burst underwater breathing in stratum 5.
- **Lore:** A net-mesh of Spawnmother's renewal-coral. Smells faintly of salt and milk.

### 9.3.4 Sythrenn's Crown
- **Source:** Sythrenn (Boss 4).
- **Function:** Negates toxic spore damage in deep Verdancy.
- **Lore:** The crown of pistils, dried and braided. Still smells like the Verdancy at dusk.

### 9.3.5 Verdant Heart
- **Source:** Auriax (Boss 5).
- **Function:** Master access key to multiple Verdancy hidden chambers; required for descent.
- **Lore:** A walnut-sized seed taken from the center of Auriax's body. The Walker hears it beat sometimes. It will sprout if planted; for ending C, this is canonical.

### 9.3.6 Tide-Heart
- **Source:** Vol'thaar (Boss 6).
- **Function:** Allows extended water-traversal; opens Drowned Aphelion endgame areas.
- **Lore:** A bead of woven seawater that does not evaporate. Walking with it on changes how the Walker hears the world.

### 9.3.7 Vol'thaar's Promise
- **Source:** Vol'thaar (Boss 6, mercy choice only).
- **Function:** Single-use call for aid in a specific Act Six fight.
- **Lore:** A small, perfectly tied knot of kelp. Untying it summons Vol'thaar from the Drowned Aphelion.

### 9.3.8 Skoldur's Hammer
- **Source:** Skoldur (Boss 7).
- **Function:** Breaks ember-locked doors; high-tier melee weapon.
- **Lore:** Skoldur's pre-corruption hammer, reforged from his shattered post-corruption hammer. Brindle Quench-of-Coals performs the reforging at the Pyrenkin Bellows.

### 9.3.9 Naeren's Salt-Crown
- **Source:** Naeren (Boss 8).
- **Function:** Salt-resistance and Dawning-night vision; standard or ritual version both work.
- **Lore:** A circlet of dawning-glass, woven with salt-thread.

### 9.3.10 Wormbound Covenant Scroll
- **Source:** Naeren ritual encounter (peaceful path).
- **Function:** Unlocks a late-game NPC; alters one Diadem-Bearer pre-fight line.
- **Lore:** A scroll of Wormbound silk inscribed with a pact between the Walker and the Wormbound elders.

### 9.3.11 Choir's Resonance
- **Source:** Veyl-Aurora (Boss 9).
- **Function:** Heightens Echo-perception in the Final Spiral; required for ending C unlock.
- **Lore:** A standing chord, captured in a thin shard of auroric ice. The chord plays softly when the Walker holds it.

### 9.3.12 Shattered Diadem
- **Source:** Diadem-Bearer (Boss 11).
- **Function:** The ending key. Choosing "Break" requires it; "Restore" requires it as a sealing material; "Become" requires it as offering.
- **Lore:** Eight curved gold fragments, formerly a single diadem, formerly a pre-Inversion Aphelion Working component.

### 9.3.13 Sovereign Name Fragments x 9 (or 10 with the Drowned Crown)
- **Sources:** Each Sovereign boss drops one.
- **Function:** Collected and assembled by the Cantor of Five Bells to form a song. Completing the song unlocks the **Cantor's Compass** and a brief lore-text reveal about the Sovereigns' original nature.
- **Lore:** Each fragment is a *resonance-name* — a short tonal phrase, the Sovereign's true name. The Sovereigns have not been called by name in millennia.

### 9.3.14 The Elided Name (lore item)
- **Source:** Recovered piece-by-piece across the game by assembling Vesari tablet fragments, Diadem manifestos, and the Emberforge journal.
- **Function:** Possession alters the Diadem-Bearer's third final line and is required for ending C.
- **Lore:** The name **Vael-Iorrion** (see 08 appendix), recovered in full.

### 9.3.15 Sythrenn's Last Petal
- **Source:** Sythrenn (Boss 4, mercy-kill only).
- **Function:** Planted at the Anchor, grows a single living Verdant flower for the rest of the game.
- **Lore:** A pale petal from Sythrenn's inner body. Still alive when given.

### 9.3.16 Sword of the Last Threnos King
- **Source:** Drowned Crown (Boss 10, optional).
- **Function:** Powerful tier-5+ weapon, with a unique attack animation drawn from Vesari ceremonial fencing.
- **Lore:** The pre-Inversion king's sword, drowned with him at the Inversion, returned now.

### 9.3.17 Aelstren's Glove
- **Source:** A late-game sidequest with Aelstren the Cartographer.
- **Function:** Doubles map-exposure range; cosmetic glove.
- **Lore:** The glove of Aelstren's lost partner, returned to Aelstren by the Walker in a sidequest. Aelstren gives the *other* glove (the matching one) to the Walker as a gift. The Walker may decline. Aelstren is canonically grateful either way.

### 9.3.18 Mira's Storage-Key
- **Source:** Acquired during a Mira-no-Last-Name sidequest where the Walker helps her remember a fragment of her family.
- **Function:** Increases Anchor storage. Cosmetic key in Mira's chosen color.
- **Lore:** A small wooden token; the inscription is Mira's lost family-name, pieced together by the Walker. The lore-canonical name is provided in a separate NPC manifest (not in this lore-set).

### 9.3.19 The Cantor's Compass
- **Source:** Sovereign-Naming sidequest completion.
- **Function:** Cosmetic accessory; spins to point toward unexplored major lore content.
- **Lore:** A Vesari astrolabe, retuned by the Cantor using the full Sovereign-name song.

## 9.4 Sidequest-only items

There are dozens of smaller named items connected to NPC sidequests. They are catalogued in a separate NPC sidequest manifest and are not lore-keystoned. A representative few:

- **Brindle's Reforging Slag** (Brindle sidequest reward; cosmetic anvil decoration)
- **Hask's Lucky Hook** (Old Hask sidequest reward; fishing-luck bonus)
- **Mira's Drawing** (Mira sidequest reward; placeable Anchor decoration; appears in the credits)
- **The Cantor's First Bell** (Cantor sidequest reward; placeable, plays a single note on the Beat)

## 9.5 The Aphelion Hymn

Across the game, the Walker may collect three Hymnal Vault sheet-glyphs — fragments of the pre-Fall **Aphelion Hymn**. Each fragment is a partial musical glyph. Possession of all three, presented to the Cantor of Five Bells, unlocks a single playable performance of the Hymn at the Anchor. The performance is non-mechanical lore content. NPCs at the Anchor pause when it plays.

This is a small, dedicated cozy-lore reward.

## 9.6 Progression gating philosophy

Progression in Aetherdeep is gated by **key relics**, not by stats. The Walker can technically wander into a higher stratum at any time, but cannot proceed through certain locked passages without the prior stratum's key relic. This is consistent with sandbox-survival convention and avoids hard level-walls.

The single exception is the Diadem-Bearer's antechamber, which requires the **Elided Name** (a lore-piecing task) as well as defeating all eight prior Sovereigns. This is a soft narrative gate: it ensures players have engaged with the lore before the keystone reveal.

## 9.7 Material conversion and recycling

Lower-tier materials can be partially converted to higher tiers at crafting stations using Aphelion-shards (rare reagent). This allows late-game players to use favorite lower-tier base equipment without it becoming irrelevant. Lore-justification: the Walker's hand-resonance grows stronger through the descent; they can imbue base materials more strongly than they could at the start.

This system supports player attachment to early equipment, which fits the cozy half of the cozy-meets-funereal tone.
