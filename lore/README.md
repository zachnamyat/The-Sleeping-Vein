## One-paragraph pitch

The world of **Vyrr** was a thriving planet until a cataclysm called **The Inversion** turned it inside-out: the surface was buried, the interior exposed, and a fragment of its dying sun — **the Aphelion Sphere** — was captured at the center of the new hollow earth. Layered remnants of folded geography became distinct biomes, each sealed in chambered strata around the Aphelion. Over uncounted eons, civilizations rose and fell within the hollow: the star-mariner **Vesari**, the cave-adapted **Hollowkin**, the **Glasswright Concord** who shaped crystallized starlight, and the **Aphelion Wardens** who tended the captive sun. Now the Aphelion is dying. Its keepers have gone mad. The corrupted **Aeon Sovereigns** — once protectors of each biome — hunt the very life they once shielded. From the Aphelion's last bright shards are spun the **Echo-Walkers**: resonant beings — the player characters — summoned to descend the strata, vanquish or redeem the Sovereigns, restore the captive sun, and uncover the truth of The Inversion.

## Design rules for this lore bible

1. **No content from any other commercial game.** Every name, place, and entity here must be original.
2. **Every gameplay verb must have lore justification.** Mining isn't just mining — it's chipping through the calcified strata of folded geography. Building isn't just building — Echo-Walkers anchor reality by imposing form.
3. **Multiplayer must be diegetic.** 1–4 players is canon because the Aphelion spins more than one Echo-Walker when the danger is great. Players have no narrative reason to fight one another.
4. **Mystery before exposition.** Major reveals come from environmental storytelling, not infodumps. Tablets, murals, and ghost-echoes carry the weight.
5. **Tone: cozy-meets-funereal.** The world is a tomb. The player is the candle. This contrast is the brand.
